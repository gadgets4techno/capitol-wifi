Misconceptions

People often confuse the terms of router, switch, and access point. According to the Student Life Department: A device that connects to the network (wired or wirelessly) may not create a network of its own and a device may not interfere with Innovator's Hall network. (The person who operates said devices may not act in a malicious intent either. ;) )

For example, if you build a wall around your house, people must be able to freely pass through your wall without restriction in both directions. If people must be "inspected" or "filtered" before passing you must deconstruct you wall or allow traffic without filtering.

In terms of networking equipment, a switch "switches" or passes data between its ports without filtering to where it passes, or if it may pass. An access point works under the same principle but switches data between a wired network to a wireless network. However, access points may not be used as they create interference with the residence's supplied wireless network, and may degrade performance for other residents. Routers (wired or wireless) create said networks but may be configured to act as a switch thus, allowing traffic to flow without filtering. This setting may be labeled as switch mode in you router's settings.

Please consult the Department of Student Life for questions about the Guide to Residental Living.

Printers, Media Devices, Hotspots, etc.

Some devices such as a PS3Ⓡ/PS4Ⓡ, RokuⓇ, ChromecastⓇ, smart-television, or wirelessly enabled printer may emit a network of its own and "does not" route traffic to the Internet. These types of devices are permitted because they "cannot" route traffic to the Internet, despite the vulnerabilities these devices may unknowingly present.

A general rule to follow: If you're not using it, turn it off

Devices such as hotspots or smartphones may emit a network that routes traffic to the Internet. Be judicious, the Internet connection at Innovator's Hall is fast and your signal may cause interference and become problematic for others.
